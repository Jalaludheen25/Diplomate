Common Enquiries

Get instant answers to the most frequent questions about Dubai business setup.

Most free zone licenses can be issued within 24 to 48 hours. Mainland licenses may take 3-5 working days depending on the mandatory approvals required for specific activities.
Yes, you can have 100% foreign ownership in both Free Zones and most Mainland commercial and industrial activities under the amended UAE Companies Law.
Setup costs vary depending on the jurisdiction and business activity. Free zone packages typically start from AED 10,000, while mainland licenses may range from AED 15,000 to AED 50,000 depending on the activity type.
No, you can incorporate your company remotely. Many free zones offer digital incorporation services that allow you to complete the entire process without being physically present in the UAE.
Business owners can obtain 2-year or 3-year residence visas through their company. Investors meeting certain criteria may qualify for the 10-year Golden Visa, providing long-term residency benefits.
Yes, most free zones allow multiple business activities under a single license. Mainland licenses can also include multiple related activities, though some may require additional approvals or fees.
Annual renewal costs include license fees, office space rental, and visa renewals. Free zone renewals typically range from AED 8,000 to AED 15,000, while mainland renewals vary based on the activity type.
Most free zones have no minimum capital requirement. For mainland companies, while there's technically no minimum for most activities, banks may require proof of committed capital for account opening, typically AED 50,000 to AED 300,000 depending on the activity.
